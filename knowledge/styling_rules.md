# Styling Rules — Outfit AI Knowledge Base

## Dress Codes & Occasions

### Casual / Everyday
- Jeans, chinos, or joggers paired with t-shirts, henleys, or relaxed button-downs
- Sneakers, loafers, or casual boots
- Hoodies, sweatshirts, and casual knits are appropriate
- Prioritize comfort and personal expression

### Smart Casual
- Dark-wash jeans or chinos with a collared shirt or neat sweater
- No athletic wear or heavily distressed pieces
- Clean sneakers, loafers, or Chelsea boots
- A blazer upgrades almost any smart casual outfit

### Business Casual
- Slacks or dress chinos (no jeans)
- Button-down shirts, blouses, or polo shirts; blazers or cardigans encouraged
- Dress shoes, oxfords, loafers, or clean leather boots
- Avoid: athletic wear, flip-flops, graphic tees

### Business Formal / Professional
- Suits (solid colors or subtle patterns), or dress trousers with a blazer
- Dress shirts (white or light blue are safest); tie optional depending on workplace
- Oxford shoes, brogues, or formal loafers
- Minimal accessories; a watch is appropriate

### Cocktail / Semi-Formal
- Knee-length or just-below-knee dress for women; dark suit or sport coat + dress trousers for men
- Dress heels, strappy sandals, or elegant flats; leather dress shoes for men
- Statement accessories are appropriate at this level

### Black Tie / Formal
- Full tuxedo (black or midnight blue) for men; floor-length gown for women
- Black bow tie; formal patent leather shoes for men; formal heels for women
- Accessories should be classic and restrained

## Seasonal Dressing

### Spring (March–May)
- Light layers: cardigans, light denim jackets, unlined blazers
- Breathable fabrics: cotton, linen blends
- Colors: pastels, earth tones, classic neutrals
- Footwear: loafers, white sneakers, light ankle boots

### Summer (June–August)
- Lightweight fabrics: linen, light cotton, chambray, seersucker
- Shorts, light dresses, breezy tops
- Sandals, espadrilles, canvas sneakers
- Lighter colors that reflect heat; hat or cover-up for sun protection

### Fall (September–November)
- Layering is key: base layer + mid-layer + outer layer
- Rich, warm tones: burgundy, forest green, camel, rust, navy
- Mid-weight fabrics: wool blends, heavier cotton, corduroy, flannel
- Boots, leather shoes, and sneakers all work; trench coats, leather jackets, heavier cardigans

### Winter (December–February)
- Warmth first: wool, cashmere, heavy knits; thermal underlayers in very cold climates
- Overcoats, parkas, or puffer jackets as outer layer
- Warm accessories: scarves, hats, gloves
- Waterproof or insulated boots for wet or snowy conditions
- Rich, dark colors suit the season well

## Color Coordination

### Neutrals (Always Safe)
White, black, grey, navy, beige/camel, tan — mix freely with each other and with any accent color.

### Key Pairing Principles
- **Neutral + one pop**: Build the outfit in neutrals, add a single statement color piece
- **Analogous**: Adjacent colors on the wheel (e.g., blue + teal + green) read as harmonious
- **Complementary**: Colors opposite on the wheel (blue + orange, purple + yellow) create contrast; use one as an accent, not 50/50
- **60-30-10 rule**: 60% dominant color, 30% secondary, 10% accent — prevents visual chaos

### Reliable Combinations
- Navy + white + tan
- Grey + burgundy
- Black + white (any accent)
- Olive + navy
- Brown + cream/camel

### Mixing Patterns
- Vary scale: pair a large pattern with a small one (e.g., wide plaid + fine stripe)
- Keep one pattern dominant; the other as an accent
- Solid colors always pair safely with any pattern

## Fit Principles
- Clothes should fit the body — not too tight, not accidentally oversized
- Tailored or slim-fit silhouettes read as more polished in most contexts
- Shoulder seams should sit at the shoulder point, not drop off or pull inward
- Trouser break: slight or no break is modern; full break is classic

---

## Footwear Formality Scale
From most casual to most formal:
1. Athletic sneakers / slides
2. Lifestyle sneakers (clean, minimal)
3. Loafers / boat shoes
4. Chelsea or chukka boots
5. Derby or Oxford shoes (unstructured)
6. Brogues
7. Formal Oxford / Derby (smooth leather)
8. Patent leather formal shoes

Match footwear formality to the rest of the outfit — mismatched formality levels are the most common style error.
